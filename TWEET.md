**1/4**
> Shipping obsidian-looper — a Claude Code skill that makes Obsidian your AI project brain.
>
> Drop asks in Inbox.md as checkboxes. Claude scouts your codebase, drafts RFCs, and marks them "needs-review" in your vault — all without you typing another message. 🧵

---

**2/4**
> The key idea: Obsidian's frontmatter status property becomes a signalling protocol.
>
> You click a chip: [approved] → Claude dispatches a fleet of sub-agents to execute.
> [changes-requested] + a comment → Claude revises and re-submits.
>
> You steer entirely through your vault. No terminal. No babysitting.

---

**3/4**
> Under the hood: an Opus conductor that never does leaf work itself.
>
> Every grep, implementation, and test goes to a sub-Claude (Haiku for search, Sonnet for code, nested Opus for complex sub-orchestration). The conductor only maps, delegates, and writes Obsidian.
>
> Four bundled Workflow templates: swarm-find, fanout-review, per-subsystem-migrate, inbox-drain.

---

**4/4**
> It runs as a continuous /loop — self-paced, faster when busy, slower when idle — driven by your vault.
>
> Open source, MIT. Configurable subsystem map + verify commands. Works with any Claude Code project.
>
> Install: git clone → ./install.sh your-repo → fill in project.config.js → /loop /obsidian-looper
>
> 👉 github.com/YOUR/obsidian-looper
